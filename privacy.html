Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-16

Jaunt is built around one principle: your trip is yours, and nobody else's.

No Data Collection

Jaunt does not collect, store, or transmit personal data. There are no servers, no analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporters, and no third-party SDKs of any kind.

iCloud Is Your Identity

Jaunt has no account system. Your iCloud identity is the only identity — we never see an email, a name, or a device identifier. When you share a trip with another iPhone, the share goes through Apple's CloudKit infrastructure end-to-end, and Visionion has no access to it.

What Stays on Your Device

  • Trips — the trip you're planning, plus every leg, packing item, expense, member, and note attached to it. Stored in SwiftData on this device.
  • Settings — your appearance preference, default currency, and display name.

If you turn iCloud on, Jaunt syncs the trip data through your iCloud private database between your devices. When you share a trip with someone else, it syncs into the shared CloudKit zone for that trip — again, end-to-end through Apple.

Permissions We Ask For

  • Camera — to scan boarding passes and receipts directly into the trip (Apple Vision is fully on-device).
  • Photo Library — to attach cover photos and receipt images.
  • Location (when in use) — to map your places and pin "Where am I" in the trip.
  • Contacts — optional, to pick a member's display name from Contacts. We do not upload contacts.

Open Source for Code Audits

While the Jaunt source repository is private, every binary shipped to the App Store can be analyzed by Apple's review team. The static App Privacy form filed with Apple is consistent with everything on this page: "data not collected" across every category.

Children

Jaunt is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect any information from children (or anyone — see above).
