🔒 Through your iCloud, not our servers

Jaunt's data — every trip, every leg, every receipt — lives in your device and syncs through Apple's iCloud directly to your trip members. Visionion has no servers, never sees your data, and doesn't operate any account system. The Chrome of trip planners: we ship the binary, your iCloud does the rest.

One trip, five surfaces

Itinerary, time-zoned

Flights, trains, hotels, places, activities — day-grouped, with confirmation codes you can copy in one tap.

Group packing

Drag from 8 templates (Beach, Cold weather, Carry-on only…). Tag items as "Mine" or "Group" and assign to a member.

Receipts, OCR'd locally

Scan a receipt with the camera — Apple Vision pulls out vendor + amount, on-device.

Split, settle, export

Equal, specific, percentage, shares. Get the minimum number of transactions to close every balance. Export PDF + CSV.

Lock-screen countdown

Live Activity for the next travel leg — Dynamic Island, lock-screen rectangle, and watch complications.

Apple Watch native

Today's legs on the wrist. Next-leg countdown complication. Glance before the gate change is even announced.

Share like a Note

Tap "Share trip" → AirDrop or Messages → your group joins. No invites, no emails to enter.

Siri-ready

"Hey Siri, what's next in Jaunt?" / "Add an expense to Jaunt." Shortcuts work on lock screen and Action Button.

Built like the old Mac apps you remember

Wanderlog wants $45/year. TripIt wants $49. Splitwise wants an account. Jaunt is one $9.99 purchase, family-shareable, with a 7-day free trial. Invited members of a shared trip join for free.

The whole point: this should feel like an app you bought once, that quietly works for the next five years. Not a SaaS that emails you every month.
